Startup costs<\/h2>\n

Quality photography equipment is notoriously expensive, so you\u2019ll want to start off with the minimum: Buying a $5,000 lens doesn\u2019t make sense if your business isn\u2019t making money yet. Many professional photographers say to plan on budgeting about $10,000 to start your photography business.<\/p>\n

According to professional photographer\u00a0Austen Diamond<\/a>, \u201cbuilding slow and smart\u201d will help you stay nimble. Allow the organic growth of your business to fund gear improvements, and avoid debt if possible, he said.<\/p>\n

Based on interviews with professional photographers, here is a basic budget for starting your business, not including studio or office space. All prices are yearly estimates or one-time purchases.<\/p>\n

  • Two external drives: $120 each (keep one backup off-site)<\/li>\n
  • Computer or laptop with sufficient memory: $2,000<\/li>\n
  • Website (Wix<\/a>,\u00a0PhotoShelter<\/a>,\u00a0SmugMug<\/a>\u00a0and\/or\u00a0Squarespace<\/a>): $60+<\/li>\n
